I am a Mechanical & Computational Engineer specializing in finite element analysis, numerical simulation, and crash biomechanics. My work bridges the gap between physical experimentation and high-fidelity computational models that inform real-world safety systems.

Currently completing my M.Sc. at the Institute for Modelling and Simulation of Biomechanical Systems (IMSB), Stuttgart, I focus on applying Human Body Models (HBMs) to assess occupant injury risk in automotive crash scenarios — with specific expertise in female-representative models and frontal impact dynamics.

My engineering philosophy is grounded in numerical rigor: every result must be physically interpretable, biomechanically plausible, and validated against established tolerance limits. I bring this precision to every simulation, report, and analysis I produce.

I am actively seeking roles in automotive safety, CAE simulation, aerospace structures, or biomechanics research where computational mechanics drives impact.

Ribcage Von Mises Stress Evolution & Load Transfer Analysis
FEA LS-DYNA Thorax

Identified load-bearing rib segments, costochondral stress concentrations, and sternum redistribution pathways consistent with established rib fracture initiation mechanics.

04 — HBM Repositioning
Pre-crash Posture Conditioning via Piper Software
Piper HBM Pre-processing

Implemented a pre-crash repositioning workflow using Piper software to modify the ViVA F50 HBM's head posture by rotating the skull +5° about the Y-axis — simulating an occupant in a fatigue or distracted driving state. Exported repositioned FE nodes for crash simulation in LS-DYNA, demonstrating the sensitivity of injury metrics to initial head posture.
